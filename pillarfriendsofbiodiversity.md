# Friends of Biodiversity: The UBF Green Card, and Why Uganda's Nature Can't Wait

*Uganda shelters nearly half the world's remaining mountain gorillas, more than 1,000 bird species, and the forests, wetlands and catchments that quietly regulate the water, food and climate that millions of Ugandans depend on every day. It is a national inheritance of extraordinary value — and it is disappearing faster than we are funding its protection.*

The **Uganda Biodiversity Fund (UBF)** exists to change that. And its **Green Card programme — Friends of Biodiversity** — is how every Ugandan, and every institution that calls Uganda home, can now be part of the solution. Not as a distant donor, but as a member who can see exactly what their support achieves.

This is an invitation to belong to something worth protecting.

## The clock is running — and it's a financing problem

Conservation in Uganda does not fail for lack of knowledge about what to do. It fails for lack of sustained, predictable resources to do it.

The scale of the gap is stark:

- **Nature is underfunded by more than US $700 billion every year, globally** — the shortfall between what the world spends protecting nature and what it actually needs (CBD Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework; BIOFIN). Uganda lives this gap as a persistent, chronic conservation financing shortfall.
- **Uganda's forest cover fell from 24% of the country in 1990 to about 11% by 2015** — roughly **122,000 hectares lost every single year** (National Forestry Authority).
- **An estimated 94% of that forest loss is driven by agriculture** (Global Forest Watch) — the same sector that feeds the nation and employs the majority of its people.

Every year we wait, more of this is lost permanently. Restoration is possible; extinction is not reversible. What Uganda's biodiversity needs is not another one-off intervention, but a **standing, locally-owned base of resources** it can count on — year after year.

That is precisely what Friends of Biodiversity is built to mobilize.

## What the UBF Green Card actually is

**Friends of Biodiversity** is the membership movement of the Uganda Biodiversity Fund. The **UBF Green Card** is your membership — proof that you have chosen to stand with Uganda's nature, and a doorway to everything that membership unlocks.

It is deliberately open to everyone:

- **Individuals** — from students to executives — who want their care for nature to count for something real.
- **Institutions** — companies, schools, and organisations ready to put biodiversity at the heart of how they operate.

Membership is not charity that vanishes into a black box. It is a contribution you can **follow, verify, and be proud of** — because UBF is built on a promise of transparency.

## Where your support goes: three priorities, five windows

Every Green Card contribution advances UBF's **three core investment priorities**:

1. **Reducing the degradation of biodiversity**
2. **Climate change resilience and adaptation**
3. **Community livelihoods**

These priorities are delivered through **five strategic programme windows** — the practical channels through which your support becomes action on the ground:

- **Nature Finance & Markets** — building the innovative financing (carbon and biodiversity credits, green bonds, partnerships) that closes Uganda's conservation funding gap for good.
- **Resilient Landscapes & Catchments** — restoring degraded forests, wetlands and watersheds, especially in Key Biodiversity Areas and refugee-hosting landscapes.
- **Nature-Positive Food Systems** — helping communities farm in ways that feed families *and* restore ecosystems.
- **Circular Economy & Green Cities** — turning waste into value and putting nature at the centre of Uganda's growing towns and cities.
- **One Health & Bioeconomy** — protecting the shared health of people, animals and ecosystems, and growing Uganda's nature-based economy.

*One membership. Five fronts. A whole country's biodiversity.*

## The difference: you can see exactly where every shilling goes

Most people who want to protect nature never find out whether their money made a difference. Friends of Biodiversity is designed to break that pattern.

Every contribution flows directly to field projects across Uganda's landscapes — and it is **tracked on a shared Accountability Dashboard and audited annually.** As a member, you don't take our word for it. You watch the numbers move: **hectares restored, trees planted, communities supported.**

And your Green Card is personal. Through UBF's *"What You're Protecting"* gallery, tiers are linked to real species and places — so your membership isn't an abstraction. It has a face. *You help protect* something specific, named, and alive.

That is what accountability looks like when it is built in from the start, not bolted on.

- **Carry your Green Card digitally** — with your membership certificate and a PDF receipt, ready whenever you need them.

Individually, a single membership plants seeds. **Together, through the App, thousands of members become a sustained national fund for nature** — the kind of predictable, locally-owned financing Uganda's biodiversity has always needed and rarely had.
